# Market Risk Notes

## Odds Review

Record:

- bookmaker or exchange
- market type: 1X2, Asian handicap, totals, group winner, qualification, outright
- odds format and capture time
- implied probability
- overround or margin when all outcomes are available
- line movement and known news catalysts

## Market Failure Modes

- Public-team premium: popular teams priced shorter than fundamentals justify.
- Star-name premium: one elite player skews perception despite fitness, role, or team balance concerns.
- Host sentiment: crowd or travel advantage double-counted.
- Recency bias: one tournament warmup or friendly result moves perception too far.
- Stale injury line: odds moved before public sources update.
- Favorite tax: outright markets overprice famous contenders.
- Longshot illusion: big payout odds mistaken for value without path analysis.

## Red-Team Questions

- Does the odds move match a concrete news event?
- Is the market disagreeing with model inputs for a reason the model missed?
- Is the analyst using market odds as proof instead of one signal?
- Are Asian handicap and totals telling a different story than 1X2?
- Is the draw probability being suppressed by fan preference?
